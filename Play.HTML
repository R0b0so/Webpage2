Basic rules of soccer



Soccer, or football as it is called in certain countries, is a game played between two teams, each with the same number of players.

The team has one designated goalkeeper (often referred to as a goalie) and outfield players.

The game is played on a field, with each team trying to score goals by kicking the ball into the other team's goal.

The way to win is simple: Score more goals than the other team in a certain amount of time.




Now that you know how the game works here are some more rules to educate you more about soccer:

  • The ball must cross the goal line to score.
  • The team with the most goals wins.
  • The goalie is the only player who can touch the ball with their hands.
  • If the ball goes out of bounds, the team who didn't touch it last gets to throw or kick it back in.
  • If a player commits a foul or offense, the other team gets a free kick.



Fouls

The game of soccer has many rules and if those rules are broken the opposing team will get a penalty.

Now its time to show you rules that can lead to a penalty:

  • Tripping
  • Pushing
  • Kicking
  • Hitting
  • Touching the ball with your hands (if you aren't the goalkeeper)
  • Charging into an opponent
  • Holding an opponent
  • Tackling from behind
  • Spitting on an opponent
